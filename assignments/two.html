Understanding Web 3.0: The Next Evolution of the Internet 🌐

The internet has gone through several major transformations. From static webpages in the early days to today’s highly interactive platforms, each phase has reshaped how we communicate, work, and build technology. The next phase, often called Web 3.0, aims to fundamentally change who owns and controls the internet.

From Web 1.0 to Web 3.0

The first generation of the web, often referred to as Web 1.0, was mostly read-only. Users could browse websites, but interaction was limited. Then came Web 2.0, the era we live in today. Social platforms, cloud services, and user-generated content dominate this phase. Major companies like Google, Meta, and Amazon built platforms where users create and share content—but the companies control the infrastructure and much of the data. Web 3.0 proposes a different model: a decentralized internet where users own their data and digital assets.
